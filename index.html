The Problem

Healthcare gaps that cost lives

India's healthcare system faces systemic challenges that AI and digital infrastructure can begin to address responsibly.

‚ö†Ô∏è

Missed Medications

Patients managing chronic conditions like diabetes, hypertension, or TB frequently miss doses, often without realising the cumulative risk. Reminders alone are not enough; context aware adherence support is essential.

Studies suggest that 30‚Äì50% of patients with chronic diseases do not take medications as prescribed across many parts of India.
üö®

Emergency Response Gaps

In rural and semi-urban India, the time between symptom onset and hospital arrival is critical. Patients often lack structured guidance on what to do, worsening outcomes before care begins.

In many rural regions of India, emergency response times can exceed 45 minutes, which is critical for time-sensitive conditions such as stroke or myocardial infarction.
üìÇ

Fragmented Health Records

Most Indians carry paper records, lose test reports, or repeat investigations unnecessarily. Without a unified, patient-owned health record, every clinical encounter starts from scratch.

It is estimated that less than 20% of the population currently has access to structured digital health records.

Our Story

Why we started Curalyf

Curalyf was born from a simple observation: India's healthcare system is vast, skilled, and under resourced, and technology, when applied thoughtfully, can bridge gaps that infrastructure alone cannot.

Our founders, a medical student, a systems engineer, and a quant analyst, came together with a shared belief: preventive care and personalised health management should not be a luxury. It should be accessible to every Indian, urban or rural.

We are building Curalyf to be a trusted layer in India's digital health ecosystem, not another app competing for attention, but infrastructure that makes healthcare safer, more continuous, and more human.
